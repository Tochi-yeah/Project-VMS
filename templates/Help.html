Frequently Asked Questions

For System Users (Dashboard & Scanning)

What's the difference between the "Request" page and the "Logs" page?

A: The "Request" page is for *registrations*. It shows all visitors who have registered, including those who haven't arrived yet. You use this page to find visitors by name.

The "Logs" page is for *history*. It shows the permanent record of all check-ins and check-outs that have already happened.

What is a unique_code?

A: The unique_code is the **permanent ID** for a specific visitor. This code is inside their QR code and is what you scan. The system uses this code to find the visitor and all their past visits.

What's the difference between a unique_code and a group_code?

A: A unique_code (like A8B2C4D6) belongs to **one person**.

A group_code (like grp_XyZ123) is given when you register a whole group at once (e.g., from an Excel upload). Scanning this one code gives you the option to check in **all members** of that group simultaneously.

What is the "global scan" feature?

A: You can scan a QR code from any page of the dashboard (Dashboard, Logs, Analytics, etc.). As long as you are not clicked into a search bar or form, the system will "hear" the scan and automatically begin the check-in or check-out process.

How do I check in a visitor?

A: You have three ways:
1. Global Scan (Fastest): From any page, just scan the visitor's QR code. The system will either check them in or open a pop-up to confirm their purpose.
2. Manual Code Entry: On the Request page, type the visitor's unique_code into the "Visitor Code Check-In" box and press "Submit".
3. Request List Button: On the Request page, find the visitor's name in the table and click the "Check-In" button. This will check them in instantly with the purpose they registered for.

A visitor forgot their QR code. How do I check them in?

A: Go to the Request page. Use the search bar to find their name. Once you find them in the table, click the "Check-In" button next to their name.

How do I check a visitor *out*?

A: Simply scan their QR code again using the global scanner. The system sees they are currently "Checked-In" and will automatically log their "Check-Out".

A large group just arrived. What's the fastest way to register them?

A: On the Request page, click the "Download Format" button. This gives you an Excel template. Fill it out with their names, phones, etc., and then use the "Upload" button to register them all at once.

How do I check in an entire group at once?

A: You can either scan the "Group QR Code" (if the leader has it) or, on the Request page, find the group row and click the "Check-In" button for the entire group.

What do "Approved By," "Checked-In By," and "Checked-Out By" mean?

A: "Approved By" is the admin who originally approved the visitor's *first-ever* registration.

"Checked-In By" and "Checked-Out By" are the system users (guards) who were logged in when they physically scanned the visitor's code at the gate.

What does the "Gate Role" (e.g., Gate 1) do?

A: Your "Gate Role" is set in your Settings page profile. When you check a visitor in or out, the system automatically records which gate you are assigned to (e.g., "Gate 1") in the log. This helps track visitor entry and exit points.

How do I get a copy of the visitor logs?

A: Go to the Logs page. You can use the date and search filters to find the data you need, then click the "Export Data" button to download an Excel (.xlsx) file of the current view.

What is the difference between the stats on the Dashboard?

A:
• Visitors Today: The total number of unique visitors who have *checked in* at least once today.
• Currently Checked In: The number of visitors who are *still inside* the campus right now (they have a "Checked-In" log but no matching "Checked-Out" log for today).
• Registered Today: The number of new visitors who *pre-registered* today via the online forms. This number is not related to check-ins.

For Visitors (Registration Page)

How do visitors register?

A: Visitors can use the public Visitor Registration form or the "Multiple Registration" form for groups.

Do visitors need an email address?

A: No. The form has a "No Email" checkbox they can use.

Can visitors reuse their QR code?

A: Yes. A visitor's individual QR code is permanent. When they scan it on a new visit, they will be prompted to confirm their purpose.
